Scripture says of all those things, the nÄá¸Ä«s, &c., that they are the place of deep sleep; and those statements we must combine into one, as the hypothesis of option would involve partial refutation 3. The assertion

It cannot, moreover, be said that the soul is at any time not united with Brahman–for its true nature can never pass away–; but considering that in the state of waking and that of dreaming it passes, owing to the contact with its limiting adjuncts, into something else, as it were, it may be said that when those adjuncts cease in deep sleep it passes back into its true nature.
