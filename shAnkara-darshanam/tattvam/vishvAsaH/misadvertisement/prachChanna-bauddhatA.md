## Philosophical similarities
shUnya-vivartavAda is bauddha, and that it is the original vivartavAda. It was modified very slightly to form "nirguNa-brahma-vivarta-vAda".

Both open and crypto bauddhas think that the in the first instance, a nirvisheSha object is grasped, and illusory "vikalpas" are grasped in the next instance. 

Both open and crypto bauddhas think that veda is illusory and born out of the blemish of avidyA, like as everything else in this transactional world. Yet, the latter illogically privilege the abheda bits of veda over other bits and the shUnyavAdi texts.

### बौद्धोलेखाः
Gaudapada: c. 500–550 CE (early 6th century); dated partly via citations by the Buddhists below.  
Bhavaviveka (Bhāviveka/Bhavya): c. 500–570 CE (mid-6th century); early Madhyamaka philosopher who quoted Gaudapada.  
Śāntarakṣita: c. 725–788 CE; influential 8th-century Buddhist scholar who cited Gaudapada's kārikās.  
Kamalaśīla: c. 740–795 CE; disciple of Śāntarakṣita and also referenced Gaudapada.
