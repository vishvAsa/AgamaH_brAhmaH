## Philosophical
### avidyA origin
#### वार्त्तिक प्रस्थान of सुरेश्वर

- Brahman is the material cause of the world. Not mAyA
- The locus of avidyA is brahman, not the jIvas
- avidyA is one, and not many
- mahAvAkyas of upaniSats can produce immediate cognition of self as brahman. No deep dhyAnAbhyAsa (meditation) on the vAkyas needed.

#### विवरण प्रस्थान of पद्मपाद

- avidyA (i.e. mAyA) is the material cause of the world
- avidya rests on brahman, but acts on the jIvas
- jIvas are mere reflections (pratibimba) of brahman in the mind

#### भामती प्रस्थान of वाचस्पति मिश्र

- brahman is the material cause of the world
- avidyA does not rest with brahman, but the jIvas
- avidyA is plural just as jIvas are plural
- mahAvAkyas do not produce immediate cognition of the ultimate reality. Deep sAdhanA / meditation is required

#### mUlAvidyA nirAsa
Also there is the hoLenarasipura sachchidAnandendra sarasvatI school, which rejects mUlAvidyA. 

### References
Swami Harshananda of RK Math discusses these doctrinal divides that appeared within the अद्वैत fold in the centuries following the passing of शङ्कर.
