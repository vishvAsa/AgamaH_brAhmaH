## shankara bhAShya challenge
**Challenge statement** - Show any sentence from shAnkara bhAShya where "parameshvara", by itself, unambiguously refers directly to nirguNa brahman (SB) without going via saguNa brahman (NB). 

Many shAnkarans fumble over this simple fact.  
Video - [YT](https://youtu.be/bJl8wumOIFw)

I am not disputing that various passages may be teaching about nirguNa-brahman or ekAtmakatA. Rather, my simple point is just this - "parameshvara" nowhere refers to nirguNa-brahman directly, without going through saguNa-brahman.

## Etymology
The fact that there is "parama" (highest) and "Ishvara" (lording over) makes it abundantly clear that saguNa-brahma is referred to by the word parameshvara. nirguNa

- v subrahmanya went bonkers and claimed "स्यात्परमेश्वरस्यापीच्छावशान् मायामयं रूपं साधकानुग्रहार्थम् ... Here, in that bhashya, Parameshwara is NB." Details in [YT](https://youtu.be/bJl8wumOIFw).
- "निरस्तसर्वविशेषं पारमेश्वरं" obviously doesn't cut it - it explicitly deletes visheSha from parameshvara. Details in [YT](https://youtu.be/bJl8wumOIFw).
- "आत्मेत्येव परमेश्वरः प्रतिपत्तव्यः ।" 
  - Details in comments under [YT](https://youtu.be/bJl8wumOIFw).
  - भामती , for example, understands "आत्मेत्येव परमेश्वरः प्रतिपत्तव्यः  । " as meaning "श्वेतकेतोरात्मैव परमेश्वरः प्रतिपत्तव्यो न तु श्वेतकेतोर्व्यतिरिक्तः परमेश्वरः । ". So, it equates shvetaketu's Atman with parameshvara (presumably owing to both of them being actually nirguNa brahman). Here, shvetaketu's Atman in jIvAtmA, while परमेश्वर is saguNa-brahma only.
