Vaṇśaṭhakōpadayā

## मङ्गलम्
पद्मानायक पादपद्मयुगले पर्यायभृंगात्मना  
चेतो मे चिर-पक्षपात-विभवाद् आस्वादम् आसीदतु ।  
अध्यात्म-श्रुति-दुग्ध-सिन्धु-लहरी संचार-सम्भावितैः  
हंस-श्रीर् अधिगम्यते सुकृतिभिर् यत्रोल्लसद्-बुद्धिभिः ॥

उद्गृह्णतीमुपनिषत्सु निगूढमर्थं चित्ते निवेशयितुमल्पधियां स्वयं नः ।  
पश्येम लक्ष्मणमुनेः प्रतिपन्नहस्तां उन्निद्रपद्मसुभगामुपदेशमुद्राम् ॥

## Intro
Of late there has been a tendency among certain neo cults and modern Advaitic thinkers like [here](https://adbhutam.wordpress.com/2018/10/02/niralambopanishat-a-few-select-passages/) to quote from certain dubious Upaniṣads which they claim is a part of the “108” listed in the Muktikā canon. We attempt to analyse the legitimacy of such claims and draw a line between true Śrutis and bogus ones.

## 108 upaniShat paxa
As per the Śrutis themselves, the Vedas are infinite – – “अनन्ता वै वेदाः”, “anantā vai vedāḥ” and every śākhā has at least 1 Upaniṣad associated with it. Therefore the proponents of those claiming 108 Upaniṣads claim that some of these from 108 are a part of these lost śākhās. The main source for these – the Muktikā is said to be a part of the Śukla Yajurveda.  

## Objections
Such claims can be objected by the following:-  

### Non mention
1\.  First of all none of the old Ācāryas of various systems of
    philosophy such as Śaṅkara, Rāmānuja, Madhva, Vallabha etc have
    claimed that there are 108 main Upaniṣads.  
The very first one
    possibly to mention that the Upaniṣads are 108 in number is Upaniṣad
    Brahmayogin who belongs to the 18th century – who lived just after
    the Mughal invasion in South India.  

### Spurious upaniShats
2\.  There are very well known spurious texts which got called as
    Upaniṣads – such as the Allopaniṣad which are rejected even by sane
    Vaidikas themselves.  

### Recensions and corruption
3\.  Many of the texts mentioned in the 108 are known to have many
    recensions that are completely different and corrupted. 

For example
    the Tejobindu Upaniṣad has just 1 chapter in some manuscripts –
    unlike the one present now. No critical edition has been considered
    for the same.  

### Persian translation misses
4\.  There is said to be a Persian translation of Upaniṣads by Dara
    Shikoh who had collected these Upaniṣads. And only 50 odd of them
    have been put. It has to be noted that many of these Upaniṣads cited
    now were not even mentioned by Dara Shikoh.  

### Medieval Indian non-notice
5\.  Not only Persian or foreign translators – even Indian logicians and
    debators such as Madhusūdana Sarasvatī or Appayya Dīkṣita have never
    quoted these which could have apparently given them an upper hand
    over Dvaitins and Viśiṣṭādvaitins.  
Either we should say that they
    were completely ignorant of the existence of such texts which later
    brilliant minds of the 18th and 19th century found out – or that
    these texts did not exist in the first place – possibly made by them
    to defend their doctrines as a last ditch effort.  

### Omission of legitimate upaniShats
6\.  There are Upaniṣads such as the Pāramātmika Upaniṣad among the
    Vaikhānasas which are not considered as part of the 108 so called
    Upaniṣads in spite of being present in their adhyayana paramparā.
    Again this should be reasoned out.  

### Questions
Is there any open evidence before 500 years of someone claiming there are 108 Upaniṣads or quoting from the Muktikā?

What is the logic behind not quoting scriptures with explicit quotations in debates earlier?

### Missing quotations
In the present edition of 108 Upaniṣads there are some statements not found in spite of being quoted by people such as Appayya Dīkṣita. 

### purANa-like material
There are also spurious texts written along the lines of Itihāsa Purāṇas – especially in Mahopaniṣad \[which many like Vedānta Deśika tells was partly lost by his time\]. What again is the logic for this?

### Number
If there were 1000+ śākhās as many claim, shouldn’t the Upaniṣad count also be 1000+ instead of 108? Why show partiality and come to this number 108?

Counter Objections considered

## Muktikā Upaniṣad quotes?
Against this some claim that the Muktikā Upaniṣad was quoted by Vidyāraṇya, Deśika among others. We regard this as being preposterous and examine their claims regarding this.

Quotations from the Jīvanmukta Viveka:

>
> वासनाहीनमप्येतच्चक्षुरादीन्द्रियं स्वतः । प्रवर्तते बहिः स्वाऽर्थे वासनामात्रकारणम् ॥”

Analysing this:

First of all Vidyāraṇya does not mention that this is from the Muktikā Upaniṣad. Secondly one can easily verify for themselves that these ślokas are in fact taken from Smṛtis and other miscellaneous literature.

The first śloka is taken from a Nāṭaka called Citrabhārata of Kṣemendra as is found [here](https://sa.wikisource.org/wiki/%E0%A4%AA%E0%A5%83%E0%A4%B7%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%A0%E0%A4%AE%E0%A5%8D:%E0%A4%95%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%B5%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%AF%E0%A4%AE%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%B2%E0%A4%BE_(%E0%A4%9A%E0%A4%A4%E0%A5%81%E0%A4%B0%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%A5%E0%A5%8B_%E0%A4%97%E0%A5%81%E0%A4%9A%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%9B%E0%A4%95%E0%A4%83).pdf/%E0%A5%A7%E0%A5%AC%E0%A5%AF).  
The second śloka can be found in the Uttara Gītā (Can be searched [here](https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_giitaa/uttaragiitaa.html)) and Cāṇakya Nīti.  
The final śloka too is found elsewhere in the Yoga & Laghu Yoga Vāsiṣṭha ( Yogavāsiṣṭhe 5.52.59; Laghuyogavāsiṣṭhe 24.70).

Therefore the hetu that he quoted keeping the Muktikā Upaniṣad in mind is inconclusive.


Elsewhere too certain ślokas are attributed to the Muktikā but these too are laughable as we see here:

> तथा च स्मृत्यन्तरं –
>

> ॥


We can see that the author of the Jīvanmuktiviveka clearly mentions that it is a Smṛti source. The author clearly is unaware of the Muktikā Upaniṣad as a Śruti – as while quoting Śruti has greater prābalya than Smṛti and hence he should have mentioned this.

However the editor of the work, desperate to prove the authenticity of the Muktikopaniṣad puts this too as a quote – indirectly exposing the so called Upaniṣad as a work of some person.

### Commentaries
Even commentaries trace it to the same – it is indeed shocking as to how commentaries can miss out on attributing it to Śruti but rely on texts like Yoga Vāsiṣṭha and other Smṛtis – again pointing out the dubious nature of this work. 

Incidentally this hints to its nature as being plagiarized from the Yoga, Laghu Yoga Vāsiṣṭha mixing it up in the middle with passages found elsewhere in the other Upaniṣads and Kāvyas - this possibly being the root cause for confusion and misattribution. 

One can verify this for oneself by searching for verses in the Muktikā and comparing them with ones in Yoga Vāsiṣṭha – chances are highly to find a match.

Even though the source isn’t explicitly mentioned by Śaṅkara as being Kaṭharudra – the author hastily goes to the conclusion that this is indeed from this Upaniṣad mentioned by Muktikā. 

One should note that there is also a Kaṭhaśruti Upaniṣad which is much shorter than the above Kaṭharudra and contains the same passage as quoted by Śaṅkara without the additional verses quoted by the above person. Therefore again as in the case of Muktikā the inference becomes inconclusive.

## Mahopaniṣad dubiousness
Furthermore even the version of Mahopaniṣad published today is of dubious nature barring the start – with various chapters looking straight out of Purāṇic texts.  

### Missing parts
There is said to be a Brahma Sūktam – another starting with sarvasya vaśinam – as being part of the work as quoted by Deśika:

> अत्र ब्रह्मसूक्तमिति महोपनिषत् मध्यगता “सहस्रशीर्षे”त्यादयः एकोनविंशति मन्त्राः । तत
> उपरितनाष्टौ मन्त्राः “सर्वस्य वशिनम्” इत्यादयाः । तत्र “दक्षिणे तु भुजे विप्र” इति
> पञ्चमो मन्त्रः। एक एव नारायण इति महोपनिषदादयो मन्त्र.. “विष्णुनात्तमश्नन्ति
> विष्णुना पीतं पिबन्ति विष्णुना घ्रातं जिघ्रन्ति विष्णुरसितं रसयन्ति तस्माद्विद्वांसो
> विष्णूपहृतं भक्षेयुः”


These are also quoted by other Dharmaśāstra kāras such as Vaidyanātha Dīkṣita in the Smṛtimuktāphala.

However in today’s version of the Mahopaniṣad – we just find the first chapter matching with whatever quotations are found in those of Pūrvācāryas and not statements such as “viṣṇunā pītaṃ pibanti viṣṇunā ghrātaṃ jighranti…”. 

### Purāṇic flavor
The current version from the 2nd chapter containing ślokas in the form of Purāṇic verses have not been quoted by any ancient source. 

### No dispute on quotations
Parallelly there has not been any objections to the quotations by Vedānta Deśika even from Śaivādvaita scholars such as Appayya Dīkṣita. This leads to the conclusion that the work was most likely lost in the middle possibly due to invasions and has been corrupted in its present form.

## Tejobindu Upaniṣad
Similarly – the Tejobindu Upaniṣad has been published with several chapters – however some manuscripts of the same show only 14 verses as being part of the work which was published as part of the 60 Upaniṣads by Paul Deussen. This again casts doubts on the authenticity of the version present now.

## Doctoring
Śrī Vedānta Deśika mentions about the doctoring of various lesser known Upaniṣad texts which were lost during his time in Śatadūṣaṇī –

> “यानि चान्यानि वाक्यानि सम्प्रतिपन्नश्रुतिस्मृतिष्वदृश्यमानानि
> स्वाचारानुरूपमतपरिचर्यया केषुचिदप्रसिद्धेषु वा नष्टकोशेषु वा अनिरूपितमूलाग्रेषु वा पुराणेषु प्रक्षिप्य पठन्ति पापिष्ठाः; तानि प्रत्यक्षश्रुत्यादिपरिशीलनशालिनीषु
> गरिष्ठगोष्ठीसु नावकाशं लभन्ते । ”+++(5)+++

We also note that even works authored by some people – like Śaṅkara were passed on as Upaniṣads such as this

Maṭhāmnāyopaniṣat – another ridiculous attempt to pass sectarian hagiography as an upaniṣat – [https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_upanishhat/maThAmnAyopaniShat.html](https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_upanishhat/maThAmnAyopaniShat.html)

Śaṅkara’s own Nirvāṇa Śataka (advertised as AtmaShaTkopaniShat) – [https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_z_misc_shankara/aatmasha.html](https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_z_misc_shankara/aatmasha.html)

One Advaitabhāvanopaniṣad attributed to One Sadāśiva :[https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_upanishhat/advaitabhAvanopaniShat.html](https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_upanishhat/advaitabhAvanopaniShat.html)

We also find that many of these so-called Upaniṣads in the list directly plagiarize verses from that of Śaṅkara – with no vyākhyāna kartā identifying them as Śrutis. 

For example look at the following verses from the Avadhūta and Ātmabodha “Upaniṣad”:

> न निरोधो न चोत्पत्तिर्न बद्धो न साधकः । न मुमुक्षुर्न वै मुक्तो इत्येषा परमार्थता ॥
>
> निष्कले निष्क्रिय शान्ते निरवद्ये निरञ्जने । अद्वितीये परे तत्त्वे व्योमवत्कल्पना कुतः॥

These are directly found in the Vivekacūḍāmaṇi as verses 573 and 574 without any acknowledgement of being from the Śruti. The below verse again found in the Ātmabodha “Upaniṣad”:

> न मे बन्धो न मे मुक्तिर्न मे शास्त्रं न मे गुरुः । मायामात्रविकासत्वान्मायातीतोऽहमद्वयः
> ॥

Is directly found in this Advaitic work Svātmaprakāśikā here again indicating widespread plagiarism by certain scholars who attempted to pass the sayings attributed to Śaṅkara as Śruti.


## On Vaiṣṇava upaniShats
However, needless is to say that if any Upaniṣadic work has not been quoted by Pūrvācāryas even if it supports Vaiṣṇava Siddhānta it will not be considered as Śruti. Such texts have as much validity as the purported Allopaniṣad among Śiṣṭas. Therefore works such as Tripādvibhūti Nārāyaṇopaniṣad, Nārāyaṇa Tāpinī, Gopāla Tāpinī, Veṅkaṭa Tāpanīya among others may not be necessarily taken as Śruti – nor are they being quoted in any honest debates on Vedānta by Viśiṣṭādvaitins. 

This applies equally to other Sampradāyas also. Those accusing madhvas of inventing hiterto unheard shrutis must retrospectively look at their own modern day quotations too.

## Conclusion
Hence it stands that the current number of Upaniṣads as 108 is concocted and is not to be accepted as genuine Śruti.
