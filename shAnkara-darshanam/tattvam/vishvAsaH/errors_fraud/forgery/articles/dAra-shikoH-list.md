No, that is not right. Dara Shikoh had collected 52 Upanishads in his
'Sirr-i-Akbar', and nowhere did he mention Muktika Upanishad. I had
pulled this list a couple of years back, when a learned friend said
that this is the first reference to sharabha upanishad (which isn't
the case either) -

The 52 Upanishads that he quotes are --

Book 1 (from Rgveda):
1. Aitareya
2. Kaushitaki
3. Bashkala *

Book 2 (from Yajurveda) :
4. Shivasankalpa Upanishad
5. Shatarudriya Upanishad
6. Brihadaranyaka
7. Maitri
8. Svetashvataara
9. Ishavasya
10. Tadeva Upanishad (!!)
11. Maha-narayana
12. Bhrigavalli
13. Purushasukta upanishad (!! Is this mudgala Upanishad possibly)
14. Anandavalli Upanishad (!!) *
15. Chhagaleya Upanishad

Book 3 (from Samaveda)
16. Chandogya Upanishad

Book 4 (from Atharvaveda)
17. Mundaka
18. Sarva Upanishad
19. Narayana Upanishad
20. Atharvashira Upanishad
21. Hamsananda Upanishad
22. Prashna Upanishad
23. Dhyanabindu
24. Mahaa
25. Atmaprabodha
26. Kaivalya
27. YogashikhA
28. Yogatattva
29. AtharvashikhA
30. Atma
31. Brahmavidyaa
32. Amritabindu *
33. Tejobindu *
34. Garbha
35. Jaabala
36. Mandukya Upanishad
37. Pingala Upanishad
38. Chulika Upanishad
39. Paramahamsa Upanishad
40. Arunika Upanishad
41. Kena Upanishad
42. Kathaka Upanishad
43. Kshurika Upanishad
44. Mrityulangula Upanishad
45. AmritanAda
46. Taraka
47. Pranava ('taraka' means same as 'praNava'. but there are two upanishads).
48. Arsheya
49. Shaunaka Upanishad
50. Narsimha Upanishad
51. Vamautaravaani Upanishad
52. Gopalautaravaani Upanishad

Dara Shikoh had collected these from his team of pandits across the
country. It is clear that muktika, sharabha, raadhika and so many
other 'upanishads' are later fabrications.

The above information is given in a work titled 'Dara Shikoh - Life
and works' authored by Bikramjit Hasrat. It is available online at
