Intro

Of late there has been a tendency among certain neo cults and modern Advaitic thinkers like here to quote from certain dubious Upaniṣads which they claim is a part of the “108” listed in the Muktikā canon. We attempt to analyse the legitimacy of such claims and draw a line between true Śrutis and bogus ones.

108 upaniShat paxa

As per the Śrutis themselves, the Vedas are infinite – – “अनन्ता वै वेदाः”, “anantā vai vedāḥ” and every śākhā has at least 1 Upaniṣad associated with it. Therefore the proponents of those claiming 108 Upaniṣads claim that some of these from 108 are a part of these lost śākhās. The main source for these – the Muktikā is said to be a part of the Śukla Yajurveda.

Objections

Such claims can be objected by the following:-

Non mention

1. First of all none of the old Ācāryas of various systems of philosophy such as Śaṅkara, Rāmānuja, Madhva, Vallabha etc have claimed that there are 108 main Upaniṣads.
The very first one possibly to mention that the Upaniṣads are 108 in number is Upaniṣad Brahmayogin who belongs to the 18th century – who lived just after the Mughal invasion in South India. +++(5)+++

Spurious upaniShats

2. There are very well known spurious texts which got called as Upaniṣads – such as the Allopaniṣad which are rejected even by sane Vaidikas themselves.

Recensions and corruption

3. Many of the texts mentioned in the 108 are known to have many recensions that are completely different and corrupted.

For example the Tejobindu Upaniṣad has just 1 chapter in some manuscripts – unlike the one present now. No critical edition has been considered for the same.

Persian translation misses

4. There is said to be a Persian translation of Upaniṣads by Dara Shikoh who had collected these Upaniṣads. And only 50 odd of them have been put. It has to be noted that many of these Upaniṣads cited now were not even mentioned by Dara Shikoh.

Counter Objections considered

Muktikā Upaniṣad quotes?

Against this some claim that the Muktikā Upaniṣad was quoted by Vidyāraṇya, Deśika among others. We regard this as being preposterous and examine their claims regarding this.

Quotations from the Jīvanmukta Viveka:

…?

The following is quoted by Vidyāraṇya which some claim is directly from the Muktikā:

“बहुशास्त्रकथाकन्थारोमन्थेन वृथैव किम् । अन्वेष्टव्यं प्रयत्नेन तत्त्वज्ञैर्ज्योतिरान्तरम् ॥

अधीत्य चतुरो वेदान्सर्वशास्त्राण्यनेकशः । ब्रह्मतत्त्वं न जानाति दर्वी पाकरसं यथा ॥

वासनाहीनमप्येतच्चक्षुरादीन्द्रियं स्वतः । प्रवर्तते बहिः स्वाऽर्थे वासनामात्रकारणम् ॥”

Analysing this:

First of all Vidyāraṇya does not mention that this is from the Muktikā Upaniṣad. Secondly one can easily verify for themselves that these ślokas are in fact taken from Smṛtis and other miscellaneous literature.

The first śloka is taken from a Nāṭaka called Citrabhārata of Kṣemendra as is found here.
The second śloka can be found in the Uttara Gītā (Can be searched here) and Cāṇakya Nīti.
The final śloka too is found elsewhere in the Yoga & Laghu Yoga Vāsiṣṭha ( Yogavāsiṣṭhe 5.52.59; Laghuyogavāsiṣṭhe 24.70).

Therefore the hetu that he quoted keeping the Muktikā Upaniṣad in mind is inconclusive.

Elsewhere too certain ślokas are attributed to the Muktikā but these too are laughable as we see here:

तथा च स्मृत्यन्तरं –

“लोकवासनया जन्तोः शास्त्रवासनयापि च । देहवासनया ज्ञानं यथावन्नैव जायते” ॥

(मुक्तिकोपनिषदि २.२; सूतसंहितायां यज्ञवैभवखण्डे पूर्वार्धे १४.१५; विवेकचूड़ामणौ २०२) ॥

We can see that the author of the Jīvanmuktiviveka clearly mentions that it is a Smṛti source. The author clearly is unaware of the Muktikā Upaniṣad as a Śruti – as while quoting Śruti has greater prābalya than Smṛti and hence he should have mentioned this.

However the editor of the work, desperate to prove the authenticity of the Muktikopaniṣad puts this too as a quote – indirectly exposing the so called Upaniṣad as a work of some person.
Elsewhere in spite of Vidyāraṇya mentioning explicitly that the verses are written by one Sarvānubhavayogin the editor puts:

तदुभयं सर्वानुभवयोगिना दर्शितम्-

“चित्तैकाग्र्याद्यतो ज्ञानमुक्तं समुपजायते । तत्साधनमतो ध्यानं यथावदुपदिश्यते” ॥ – (मुक्तिकोपनिषदि २.४९) ॥

“विलाप्य विकृतिं कृत्स्नां सम्भवव्यत्ययक्रमात् । परिशिष्टं च सन्मात्रं चिदानन्दं विचिन्तयेत्” ॥ इति । – (मुक्तिकोपनिषदि २.५०) ॥

“ब्रह्माकारमनोवृत्तिप्रवाहोऽहङ्कृतिं विना । सम्प्रज्ञातसमाधिः स्याद्ध्यानाभ्यासप्रकर्षजः” ॥ – (मुक्तिकोपनिषदि २.५३) इति च ॥

The final śloka is quoted in the Sāṅkhyatattvakaumudī Ṭīkā and ascribed to an abhiyukta – or a trustworthy person (proof) again showing that it wasnt regarded as a Śruti and is most likely taken from some other older book and inserted in this Muktikā.

The same Yogin is quoted by Śaṅkara too in his Upadeśa Sāhasrī –

तं च भगवत्पादा उदाजह्रुः “दृशिस्वरूपं गगनोपमं परं सकृद्विभातं त्वमेकमक्षरम् । अलेपकं सर्वगतं यदद्वयं तदेव चाहं सततं विमुक्त ओम् ॥ – (उपदेशसाहस्र्यां १०.१) ॥

“दृशिस्तु शुद्धोऽहमविक्रियात्मको न मेऽस्ति कश्चिद्विषयः स्वभावतः । पुरस्तिरश्चोर्ध्वमधश्च सर्वतः सम्पूर्णभूमा त्वज आत्मनि स्थितः ॥ – (तत्रैव १०.२) इति ॥

“अजोऽमरश्चैव तथाजरोऽमृतः स्वयम्प्रभः सर्वगतोऽहमद्वयः । न कारणं कार्यमतीव निर्मलः सदैकतृप्तश्च ततो विमुक्त ओम्” ॥ – (तत्रैव १०.३) इति ॥

Note that again this is attributed to human authorship and not as a Śruti.

Ānandagiri in his Ṭīkā on Upadeśa Sāhasrī mentions as follows: prakṛtasyāviṣayasya pratyagātmano jñānamaviṣayatvenaiva bhavatīti svānubhavānurodhena prakaṭayan prakaraṇāntaraṃ prastauti – dṛśīti

Again another author quotes a verse (in this website) from Pañcadaśī which is said to be in the Muktikā:

दर्शनादर्शने हित्वा स्वयं केवलरूपतः । य आस्ते कपिशार्दूल ब्रह्म स ब्रह्मवित्स्वयम् ॥

darśanādarśane hitvā svayaṃ kevalarūpataḥ | ya āste kapiśārdūla brahma sa brahmavitsvayam ||

The author cleverly inserts the word “kapiśārdūla” while in the Pañcadaśī it is mentioned as “yastiṣṭhati sa tu brahman brahma sa brahmavitsvayam”. This again is said to be from the Yoga Vāsiṣṭha as said here by the relevant vyākhyāna kartās themselves.

Due to the non exclusivity of such quotes we can see that any claims regarding Muktikā being quoted before remain inconclusive.

Commentaries

Even commentaries trace it to the same – it is indeed shocking as to how commentaries can miss out on attributing it to Śruti but rely on texts like Yoga Vāsiṣṭha and other Smṛtis – again pointing out the dubious nature of this work.

Incidentally this hints to its nature as being plagiarized from the Yoga, Laghu Yoga Vāsiṣṭha mixing it up in the middle with passages found elsewhere in the other Upaniṣads and Kāvyas - this possibly being the root cause for confusion and misattribution.

One can verify this for oneself by searching for verses in the Muktikā and comparing them with ones in Yoga Vāsiṣṭha – chances are highly to find a match.

Kaṭha Rudra?

Next we look at the apparent proof for other dubious Upaniṣads such as the “Kaṭha Rudra” quoted in Adbhutam’s blog here

yajñopavītaṃ vedāṃśca sarvaṃ tadvarjayedyatiḥ

यज्ञोपवीतं वेदांश्च सर्वं तद्वर्जयेद्यतिः

Even though the source isn’t explicitly mentioned by Śaṅkara as being Kaṭharudra – the author hastily goes to the conclusion that this is indeed from this Upaniṣad mentioned by Muktikā.

One should note that there is also a Kaṭhaśruti Upaniṣad which is much shorter than the above Kaṭharudra and contains the same passage as quoted by Śaṅkara without the additional verses quoted by the above person. Therefore again as in the case of Muktikā the inference becomes inconclusive.

Non acceptance as upaniShat list

While referring to the Mahopaniṣad – Vedānta Deśika discusses on its authenticity and refers to Ācāryas before Rāmānuja from his own sect as well as rival sects quoting the work :

तथा तन्मतानुसारिभिः नारायणाचार्यैरपि तत्त्वनिर्णये श्रुतितात्पर्यनिर्णयाधिकारे

This shows that in the case of Upaniṣads which are no more in the adhyayana paramparā – the only way back then was to verify whether it had been accepted in the works of rival sects as well as one’s own older teachers –
texts such as Muktikā if present would have made the verification easier or not allowed for any chance of accusation of inventing texts back during Deśika’s time.+++(5)+++

Mahopaniṣad dubiousness

Furthermore even the version of Mahopaniṣad published today is of dubious nature barring the start – with various chapters looking straight out of Purāṇic texts.

Missing parts

There is said to be a Brahma Sūktam – another starting with sarvasya vaśinam – as being part of the work as quoted by Deśika:

अत्र ब्रह्मसूक्तमिति महोपनिषत् मध्यगता “सहस्रशीर्षे”त्यादयः एकोनविंशति मन्त्राः । तत उपरितनाष्टौ मन्त्राः “सर्वस्य वशिनम्” इत्यादयाः । तत्र “दक्षिणे तु भुजे विप्र” इति पञ्चमो मन्त्रः। एक एव नारायण इति महोपनिषदादयो मन्त्र.. “विष्णुनात्तमश्नन्ति विष्णुना पीतं पिबन्ति विष्णुना घ्रातं जिघ्रन्ति विष्णुरसितं रसयन्ति तस्माद्विद्वांसो विष्णूपहृतं भक्षेयुः”

These are also quoted by other Dharmaśāstra kāras such as Vaidyanātha Dīkṣita in the Smṛtimuktāphala.

However in today’s version of the Mahopaniṣad – we just find the first chapter matching with whatever quotations are found in those of Pūrvācāryas and not statements such as “viṣṇunā pītaṃ pibanti viṣṇunā ghrātaṃ jighranti…”.

Purāṇic flavor

The current version from the 2nd chapter containing ślokas in the form of Purāṇic verses have not been quoted by any ancient source.

No dispute on quotations

Parallelly there has not been any objections to the quotations by Vedānta Deśika even from Śaivādvaita scholars such as Appayya Dīkṣita. This leads to the conclusion that the work was most likely lost in the middle possibly due to invasions and has been corrupted in its present form.

Tejobindu Upaniṣad

Similarly – the Tejobindu Upaniṣad has been published with several chapters – however some manuscripts of the same show only 14 verses as being part of the work which was published as part of the 60 Upaniṣads by Paul Deussen. (Source: TW) This again casts doubts on the authenticity of the version present now.

Deussen (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

Although the train of thoughts here is on the whole clear, the individual passages are often equally difficult and have already demanded from the Indian commentator the most jaw-breaking exegetical acrobatics. But when he e.g. in verse 9 puts up with brahmanam instead of the required brahma with the remark that here is only a confusion of gender and case (linga- vibhakti-vyatyayah), or when in verses 12 ff. he supplies śrità na vidur (subject, copula, negation and predicate) to the accusatives lobham etc., which are completely hanging in air, to make them intelligible, then we shall not follow him here as in much else but we will find ourselves facing the alternative as to whether the language feeling was already so completely blunted in the original author of these verses, or whether we are dealing with an enormously corrupt text-transmission.

The latter alternative would seem to be supported by the numerous but not better readings of the Telugu ed. in which our 14 stanzas form only the beginning of the Tejobindapanisad, which is followed by discussions in verses and prose which exceed more than thirty times the extent of our recension.]

For example look at the following verses from the Avadhūta and Ātmabodha “Upaniṣad”:

न निरोधो न चोत्पत्तिर्न बद्धो न साधकः । न मुमुक्षुर्न वै मुक्तो इत्येषा परमार्थता ॥

निष्कले निष्क्रिय शान्ते निरवद्ये निरञ्जने । अद्वितीये परे तत्त्वे व्योमवत्कल्पना कुतः॥

These are directly found in the Vivekacūḍāmaṇi as verses 573 and 574 without any acknowledgement of being from the Śruti. The below verse again found in the Ātmabodha “Upaniṣad”:

न मे बन्धो न मे मुक्तिर्न मे शास्त्रं न मे गुरुः । मायामात्रविकासत्वान्मायातीतोऽहमद्वयः ॥

Is directly found in this Advaitic work Svātmaprakāśikā here again indicating widespread plagiarism by certain scholars who attempted to pass the sayings attributed to Śaṅkara as Śruti.

On Vaiṣṇava upaniShats

However, needless is to say that if any Upaniṣadic work has not been quoted by Pūrvācāryas even if it supports Vaiṣṇava Siddhānta it will not be considered as Śruti. Such texts have as much validity as the purported Allopaniṣad among Śiṣṭas. Therefore works such as Tripādvibhūti Nārāyaṇopaniṣad, Nārāyaṇa Tāpinī, Gopāla Tāpinī, Veṅkaṭa Tāpanīya among others may not be necessarily taken as Śruti – nor are they being quoted in any honest debates on Vedānta by Viśiṣṭādvaitins.

This applies equally to other Sampradāyas also. Those accusing madhvas of inventing hiterto unheard shrutis must retrospectively look at their own modern day quotations too.
